Appointments: Outpatient appointments can be booked online through the patient portal, by calling the appointment desk at +1-800-555-0100, or in person at the main reception. New patients should bring a valid photo ID and insurance card. Same-day slots may be available for urgent but non-emergency concerns.

Appointments: Specialist referrals require a referral letter from your primary care physician unless your insurance plan allows direct access. Reschedule or cancel at least 24 hours in advance to avoid a no-show fee.

Emergency: The Emergency Department is open 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. For life-threatening conditions such as chest pain, severe bleeding, stroke symptoms, or difficulty breathing, call emergency services (911) or come directly to the ED entrance on Maple Street.

Emergency: For non-urgent issues, consider urgent care or scheduling an outpatient visit. Average ED wait times are posted on the hospital website and updated every 30 minutes.

Visiting hours: General ward visiting hours are 10:00 AM to 8:00 PM daily. ICU visiting is limited to two visitors at a time between 11:00 AM and 7:00 PM. Children under 12 must be accompanied by an adult and may have restricted access during flu season.

Insurance: The hospital accepts most major insurance plans including Medicare and Medicaid. Bring your insurance card and photo ID to every visit. Self-pay and payment plans are available; contact the billing office for estimates before elective procedures.

Insurance: Pre-authorization may be required for surgery, MRI, CT scans, and certain specialist visits. The insurance verification desk can confirm coverage Monday to Friday, 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM.

Laboratory: Blood and urine tests are performed at the main lab on the ground floor, Building A. Fasting labs should be done before 10:00 AM unless instructed otherwise. Most routine results are available in the patient portal within 24 to 48 hours.

Pharmacy: The in-hospital pharmacy is open 7:00 AM to 9:00 PM on weekdays and 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM on weekends. Prescriptions from hospital providers can be filled before discharge. External prescriptions are accepted with a valid prescription label.

Telemedicine: Video consultations are available for follow-ups, medication reviews, and minor illnesses. Book through the patient portal under Telehealth. You need a stable internet connection and a device with camera and microphone.

Medical records: Request copies of medical records through the Health Information Management office or the online records request form. Processing typically takes 5 to 10 business days. Photo ID is required for pickup or release to a designated representative.

Billing: Hospital bills are mailed within 30 days of discharge or visit. Pay online, by phone, or at the billing office in the main lobby. Financial counselors are available for patients who need help with payment plans or charity care applications.

Departments: Key departments include Cardiology, Orthopedics, Pediatrics, Maternity, Oncology, and Radiology. Directory and floor maps are available at reception and on the hospital website under Find a Department.

Vaccination: Flu shots and routine adult vaccines are offered at the outpatient immunization clinic on Tuesdays and Thursdays, 9:00 AM to 4:00 PM. Walk-ins welcome for flu season; other vaccines may require scheduling.

Contact: General inquiries: info@citygeneralhospital.org or +1-800-555-0100. Main hospital address: 100 Wellness Drive. Patient relations: patientrelations@citygeneralhospital.org, Monday to Friday, 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM.
